Global Rise in Sea Level

Global Sea Level Rise (SLR) has become a serious global threat with continued growth of greenhouse gas emissions and associated global warming.

This visualisation studies the effect of SLR on 84 coastal developing countries by looking at critical elements such as impact on population, land, GDP, and agriculture, by inundation zones projected at 1m to 5m SLR.

Effect on Global Population

Vietnam is the most seriously impacted by SLR. There is a large proportion of Vietnam's population residing in the Mekong and Red River Deltas, the most impacted area by SLR. 10.8% of Vietnam's population is impacted by a 1m SLR. This is the largest percentage among all 84 coastal countries.

Effect on Land Area

Land Area in East Asia is the most significantly impacted by SLR. For all Sea Level Rise Scenarios, East Asia is the most severely impacted region in the developing world. A major contributor is Vietnam where 16% of its country area is impacted by a 5m SLR.

Effect on Agriculture

Agricultural extent in East Asia is the most significantly impacted by SLR, where major contributors include Myanmar and Vietnam where the impact on Agricultural extent reaches 11.1% and 23.4% respectively with a 5m SLR.

Effect on GDP

The impact of SLR on GDP is slightly larger than the impact on population, because GDP per capita is generally above average for coastal populations and cities.Impact on GDP is much larger in East Asia than in any other region of the world, reaching 10.2% with a 5m SLR.
